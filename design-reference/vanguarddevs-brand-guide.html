The idea

Sec.01

Vanguard → avant-garde. The name means the front line of an advance, and it's the same word the experimental art movements of the 1920s used for themselves. The brand borrows their visual language — poster-weight type, a signal red, hard geometry, exposed structure — to say one thing: this studio builds ahead of the line.

The audience is mid-size businesses — primarily in Venezuela, extending across the Americas and the US. The promise is technical excellence. Every design decision should feel deliberate, engineered, and slightly ahead — never decorative, never soft, never templated.

Language: the brand is bilingual by design. Spanish leads; English is a full first-class version, never an afterthought. Website ships with an ES/EN toggle (ES default). Instagram captions run Spanish first, then a condensed English block after a "—EN—" divider. Never machine-translate tone: "la vanguardia" carries the brand name natively in Spanish — use it.

Voice

Sec.05

We sound like

  • Declarative and specific"We ship weekly increments you can click." Not "we deliver value-driven solutions."
  • Engineer-directPlain verbs, short sentences, real numbers. Confidence comes from precision, not adjectives.
  • Forward-facingEvery message points at what happens next — the vanguard never describes the rear.

We never sound like

  • Agency-speakNo "synergy," "solutions," "passionate," "cutting-edge," or exclamation points.
  • Hype without proofNever claim AI magic. Show the pipeline, the metric, the before/after.
  • Apologetic or softNo hedging. If we're not sure, we say what we'll do to find out.
